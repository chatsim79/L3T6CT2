Fender Richie Kotzen Signature Telecaster

This is another Japanese guitar, a signature model for the guitarist Richie Kotzen who started on the scene in the bad Poison, back in the 80s when guys playing guitar in Glam Metal bands looked like the Ladies from Baywatch, except the players had bigger hair. Totally cool with that BTW, but it was the best way of providing a visual. Unusually for the tye of music he was playing, he was VERY fond of using 1) Telecasters with 2) massive necks. This guitar was unavailable outside of Japan except for grey importers until the early 2010's, which is how i got mine. Since this, it was released globally and has proved to be a very popular model.

This is a Telecaster, with gold hardware, neither of which I'm typically fond of, but the first time I played this guitar in the store, i knew i had to have it. More modern, contemporary tones are provided via the inclusion of a single coil sized humbucker at the bridge. The massive neck was a result of Richie being a massive guy (6.5ft tall) with massive hands. He chose a large neck for this guitar purely for playing ergonomics. Fortunately it works for guys like me with baby sized hands.
