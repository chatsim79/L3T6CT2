Soldano SLO100

The Soldano Super Lead Overdrive (S.L.O) 100 is another legendary piece of guitar gear. It's pronounced "Ess, Ell Oow" by the way, not "Slow". Mike Soldano designed this in the mid 1980s and by the late 80's there was a waiting list to own one. Interestingly any major guitarist that's ever owned one of these (most of them), waited in line and paid full price as Mike didn't do sponsorship deals. These amplifiers were handbuilt, and VERY expensive because of the quality and design of components that went into them. From 1% tolerence resistors to custom designed and made transformers, it was an expensive amp to build. In the 25-30 years of production, there were probaly only 2500 of these made. Compare that to Mesa Boogie or Marshall who probably couldn't even count the amount of a particular amp they make in year, never mind 25.

The main reason for it's popularity was it was one of the original high gain (distortion) amps, i.e. an amp that didn't require any extra help from distortion or overdrive pedals. Add to that a pretty unique tonal character that is unusually articulate for so much gain, yet always stands out in the mix, and it became legendary and sought after very quickly.

Mike sold the company a few years ago, and continues to do consultation and design work for the new owners. This particular SLO100 was built during Mike's ownership in 2008, so it's considered a "mark 1", touched by the hands of Mike himself... (guitar players can be suprisingly superstitious). The version produced by the current owners is Mike approved and contains many updates he himself never had any time to make, because he spent so much time actually running the company.
